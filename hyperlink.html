Kajal Aggarwal (born 19 June 1985) is an Indian actress and model who mainly appears in Telugu and Tamil language films, in addition to a few Hindi films.[3][4][5] She has acted in more than 50 films. Aggarwal made her acting debut with the 2004 Hindi film Kyun! Ho Gaya Na... and had her first Telugu film release in 2007, Lakshmi Kalyanam. In the same year, she starred in the box office hit Chandamama, which earned her recognition. The 2009 historical fiction Telugu film Magadheera marked a turning point in her career, earning her critical acclaim. It ranks among the highest-grossing Telugu films of all time and fetched her Best Actress nominations at several award ceremonies including South Filmfare Awards.[6][7] She subsequently starred in Telugu films such as Darling (2010), Brindavanam (2010), Mr. Perfect (2011), Businessman (2012), Naayak (2013), Baadshah (2013), Govindudu Andarivadele (2014), Temper (2015) and Khaidi No. 150 (2017).[8] Kajal also played the female lead in the high-profile Tamil projects Naan Mahaan Alla (2010), Maattrraan (2012), Thuppakki (2012), Jilla (2014), Vivegam (2017) and Mersal (2017).[9] She made a comeback to Hindi cinema with Singham (2011), which was a hit, while another film Special 26 (2013) was also a box office success.[10][11] In 2020, a wax figure of Aggarwal was put on display at Madame Tussauds Singapore, making it the first of an actress from South Indian cinema.[12][13]

Early life

Aggarwal was born and brought up in a Punjabi family settled in Bombay (present-day Mumbai).[14] Her father Suman Aggarwal, is an entrepreneur in the textile business and her mother Vinay Aggarwal is a confectioner,[2] and also Kajal's business manager. Kajal has a younger sister Nisha Aggarwal, an actress in Telugu, Tamil and Malayalam cinema,[15] who is now married to Karan Valecha (Managing director Golds gyms, Asia).

She studied at St. Anne's High School, fort and completed her pre-university education at Jai Hind College.[16] She pursued her graduation in mass media, with specialisation in marketing and advertising, from Kishinchand Chellaram College.[16][17] Having harboured MBA dreams all through her growing years, she intends to achieve a post-graduation degree soon.[18]

Public recognition and critical acclaim

Aggarwal had four releases in 2009. She first starred opposite Vinay Rai in the Tamil film Modhi Vilayadu, which garnered mixed reviews and was a financial failure. She then appeared in the high budget Telugu historical drama Magadheera, alongside Ram Charan Teja, which saw her playing double roles for the first time. The film, directed by S. S. Rajamouli, received overwhelming critical acclaim, while Aggarwal, in particular, was praised for her portrayal of a princess. Aggarwal was nominated for the Filmfare Award for Best Actress in Telugu and nominated for the award for Best Telugu Actress in the South Scope Awards for her performance.[24][25] It was highly successful commercially and broke several records,[26] emerging as the highest-grossing Telugu film of all time.[27][28][29] Magadheera's success turned Aggarwal into one of the most sought-after actresses in Telugu cinema.[30] It was released again in Tamil as Maaveeran in 2011, and was also successful at the box office.[31] Her subsequent releases Ganesh Just Ganesh, opposite Ram and Arya 2 opposite Allu Arjun received mixed reviews from critics, while her performance garnered positive feedback.[32][33] Aggarwal's first 2010 release was A. Karunakaran's romantic comedy Darling, which featured her alongside Prabhas and received a favourable response, becoming a commercial success at the box office,[34] with Kajal receiving her second Filmfare nomination for Best Actress. Later that year, Aggarwal appeared in the Tamil thriller film Naan Mahaan Alla, opposite Karthi, which was based on a real-life incident and opened to positive reviews.[35] It was a box office success. The chemistry between Karthi and Aggarwal was widely praised.[36] It was later dubbed in Telugu as Naa Peru Siva in Andhra Pradesh and was a success.[37] Aggarwal's final release in 2010 was another romantic comedy Brindavanam opposite Jr. NTR and Samantha, which received critical acclaim and went on to become a commercial success,[38] while fetching Aggarwal the CineMAA Award for Best Actress. In 2011, Aggarwal was paired with Prabhas for the second time in the romantic comedy Mr. Perfect, directed by Dasaradh. The film became a critical and commercial success. Aggarwal's performance as a conservative doctor and her chemistry with Prabhas was praised by critics.[39][40] Aggarwal received her third Filmfare nomination for Best Actress in Telugu for her performance.[41] In May, she appeared in Veera, replacing Anushka Shetty[42] and starring opposite Ravi Teja for the first time.[43] The film received moderate reviews.[44]
